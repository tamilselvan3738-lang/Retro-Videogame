How to Play Classic Games

Classic arcade systems do not feature tutorials or safety parameters. To master original cabinets, you must understand mechanical micro-switches, vector trajectories, and frame windows.

  • ■
    Retro Shoot-'Em-Ups (Shmups): Focus on the center pixel of your ship. Classic titles (like Space Invaders or Galaga) calculate collisions based on a small hitbox. Move in sweeping columns, and tap fire instead of holding to maximize screen projectile limits.
  • ■
    Co-op Side-Scrolling Beat-'Em-Ups: Use vertical axis displacement. Opponents cannot attack you diagonally. Approach enemies by walking from the top or bottom edges of the screen, and execute throw combos (D-PAD DOWN + ATTACK) to throw enemies into incoming waves.
  • ■
    2D Classic Platformers: Learn momentum buffering. Inertia in classic games (e.g. platformers from the 8-bit era) is calculated on start frames. Hold down the sprint key prior to jump execution, and target platform edges using the outer pixel of the player collision box.

FILE_02 // LEADERBOARD_REGULATIONS

Leaderboard Rules

To maintain archival integrity on our Live boards, score submissions must comply with strict system-proofing standards. Fake records are purged from the mainframe.

PROOF OF PERFORMANCE AND VALIDATION PROTOCOL
  • ▶
    Video Capture Verification: All high-score submissions above 100,000 points require continuous visual capture of the screen and controls. The record must showcase the player insert token sequence and the final "Game Over" ROM state without transitions.
  • ▶
    Hardware Standards: Plays must occur on original hardware, authorized cabinets, or emulation units running at native clock-rates. Fast-forward keys, save states, and automated macros are strictly prohibited.
  • ▶
    Glitch Policies: Glitches that bypass gameplay (such as the Galaga "no fire" exploit or Pac-Man screen wrap bugs) disqualify the run. Software exploits that artificially pad scores without active play will cause entries to be removed.

FILE_03 // TOURNEY_CONSTITUTION

Tournament Guidelines

Our weekend bracket tournaments feature localized combat rules on the fighting decks. Review tournament configurations to ensure competitive parity.

  • ✚
    Bracket Structure: Tournaments run on double-elimination brackets. All matches are Best of 3 sets, except for Winner, Loser, and Grand Finals, which run on a Best of 5 standard.
  • ✚
    The Coin Drop Limit: Once a match is initialized, no additional coins/credits can be inserted. If a player runs out of lives or credits mid-match, it counts as a forfeit of the set.
  • ✚
    Button Configuration: Players have 45 seconds prior to match launch to execute button mapping tests. Lever and microswitch adjustments must be completed before starting the match.

FILE_04 // ARCADE_HISTORY

Retro Gaming History

Arcade machines were the pioneers of interactive entertainment. They shaped gaming culture, pushed technology boundaries, and established modern design paradigms.

1971 - THE FIRST SPARK
Computer Space Launches
Nolan Bushnell and Ted Dabney design the first commercial arcade game, housed in a fiberglass cabinet. It laid the foundation for the creation of Atari.
1978 - THE GOLDEN WAVE
Space Invaders Invades the World
Taito releases Space Invaders, creating a global sensation. It introduced high-score tracking, progressive difficulty, and is credited with launching the Golden Age.
1980 - THE PIXEL ICON
Pac-Man Revolutionizes Culture
Namco's maze game introduces character-driven mascot design and reaches audiences beyond traditional tech spaces, cementing gaming in popular culture.

FILE_05 // ARCADE_SOCIAL_CODE

Arcade Etiquette & Posture

Arcades are shared social grids. Respecting visual boundaries, queue protocols, and physical ergonomics optimizes the gaming experience for everyone on the floor.

  • ✔
    Queueing with Quarters: Do not crowd or hover directly behind players. Place your quarter on the bottom bezel screen frame. This signals your queue position in a classic way.
  • ✔
    Optimal Standing Stance: Align your feet shoulder-width apart, perpendicular to the control deck. Bend your knees slightly to reduce lower back strain. This stance provides maximum stability during intense button sequences.
  • ✔
    Cabinet Screen Etiquette: Avoid touching the monitor glass. CRT screens carry static charges that attract dust, and finger oils create visual distortion.

How does the token system operate?

Our arcade uses a dual payment standard. You can insert standard quarters directly into cabinet coin slots, or buy custom copper tokens at the terminal desk ($1 = 4 tokens).

Can I reserve a cabinet for private training sessions?

Yes. VIP Lounge membership grants cabinet scheduling privileges. You can reserve fighting decks or racing rigs during off-peak hours (Mon-Thu, 4 PM to 6 PM) for tournament preparation.

What is the policy regarding drinks near arcade cabinets?

All cabinets are equipped with side drink holders. Drinks must not be placed on control panels or glass screens to prevent spill damage to micro-controllers.
